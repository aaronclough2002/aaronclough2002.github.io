About

A little more about me

I’m Aaron Clough, and professionally I’ve spent much of my career leading projects, solving problems, and helping teams move complex work forward. I enjoy bringing structure to messy situations, communicating clearly, and keeping people aligned when there are a lot of moving parts.

Personally, I’m a lot more than my resume. I love my wife Tera, I enjoy spending time with our dogs, and I’m happiest when I’m active, learning something new, or working on a project that improves something around me. Tennis has been a big part of my life, and so has the mindset that comes with it — compete hard, keep improving, and don’t panic when things get messy.

I also love music, trying new foods, watching or playing just about any sport, and taking on home improvement projects. I tend to be analytical by nature, which shows up in how I work and how I think. Before the project management side of my career, I was a high school computer teacher and coach, which probably says a lot about me too — I like helping people, explaining things clearly, and finding better ways to do them.

More recently, I’ve been exploring AI through hands-on projects. Not because I’m trying to pretend to be something I’m not, but because I genuinely enjoy learning new technology and building useful things with it. That’s been one of the most fun and energizing things I’ve done in a long time.

Projects

Things I’ve built

A few practical AI projects I’ve worked on recently.

Document Knowledge Base

A RAG-style document repository that lets users upload documents, ask questions across the library, and receive grounded answers based on the uploaded content.

I also added validation rules to block certain content at upload, including forward-looking company plans, Social Security Numbers, programming code, and unreadable documents.

Live Demo GitHub

AI Meeting Summary Tool

A simple AI tool that turns meeting transcripts, project notes, or freeform updates into clear, structured summaries with key points, risks, blockers, and action items.

The goal was to make status communication easier and faster while still keeping the output polished and useful for business stakeholders.
