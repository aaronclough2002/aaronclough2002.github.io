How I Got Here

I started my career as a high school technology teacher and coach, teaching courses such as Business Information Systems, Web Design, Video Production, and Digital Media while coaching football and soccer.

I also led student teams responsible for live production at football games, including cameras and jumbotron operations. That experience built a strong foundation in leadership, communication, and organization.

From there, I entered a software development program at the University of Texas and transitioned into enterprise technology, working across project management, product ownership, technical business analysis, and delivery leadership roles.

How I Add Value

Bringing Structure to Complex Work

I’m at my best when a project has a lot of moving parts, unclear ownership, or competing priorities. I help teams organize the work, define what matters most, and move forward with clarity.

Connecting Business and Technical Teams

I naturally operate between stakeholders, product teams, and engineering. I help turn business needs into actionable work and keep people aligned on what success actually looks like.

Asking the Right Questions Early

A lot of delivery issues come from assumptions or gaps in understanding. I tend to spot those early by asking the questions that bring hidden risks and unclear expectations to the surface.

Maintaining Momentum

I focus on keeping work moving. That means removing blockers, prioritizing thoughtfully, and making progress visible so teams and leaders can make decisions with confidence.
