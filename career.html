How I Got Here

Earlier in my career, I worked as a high school technology teacher and coach. I taught courses such as Business Information Systems, Web Design, Video Production, and Digital and Interactive Media, while also coaching football and soccer.

I also served as the academic sponsor for the accounting team and computer applications team, and led a student crew that ran the cameras and jumbotron during high school football games. Looking back, that experience sharpened a lot of the same skills I still use today: communication, leadership, organization, and helping people work well together.

From there, I was accepted into a software developer training program at the University of Texas. After completing the program, I was placed on the payroll team and got the opportunity to work on the 15th floor of the UT Tower, which was a pretty memorable way to begin building experience in software and enterprise systems.

Since then, my path has included both large corporate organizations and startup environments. Along the way, I’ve worked across project management, product ownership, technical business analysis, and delivery leadership, which is why I tend to be most effective in roles where I can bridge business and technical teams.
