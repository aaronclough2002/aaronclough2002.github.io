A little more about me

The short version: I like meaningful work, clear communication, tennis, good music, trying new things, and improving systems that aren’t working the way they should.

Professionally, I’ve spent much of my career leading projects, solving problems, and helping teams navigate complexity. I like bringing structure to messy situations, keeping people aligned, and moving work forward without making things more complicated than they need to be.

Before project management became my main path, I was a high school computer teacher and coach. That experience still shows up in how I work today. I enjoy explaining things clearly, helping people connect the dots, and creating systems that make life easier instead of harder.

Personally, I’m married to my wife Tera, and she’s a huge part of my life. I also love tennis, sports in general, music of almost every kind, trying new food, and taking on home improvement projects around the house. I’m naturally analytical, so I tend to enjoy anything that involves improving, optimizing, or figuring out how something can work better.

More recently, I’ve been exploring AI through hands-on projects. I don’t pretend to be something I’m not, but I do genuinely enjoy learning new tools and applying them in practical ways. That’s been one of the most energizing things I’ve worked on in a long time.
